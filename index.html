About

I am a dedicated and driven dreamer passionate about leveraging cutting edge technology to sculpt a brighter future.

My name is Aaron Young. I am an ambitious undergraduate student at the University of Wisconsin - Madison. As a Mechanical Engineering and Computer Science major, I focus on balancing a rigorous course load and an enriching extracurricular experience. At UW, I have joined three student organizations that both enhanced and builds upon the education I received in the classroom.

Currently, I am an active member of Wisconsin Autonomous, Engineering EXPO, and Insight Wisconsin. As a board member and leader of the Autonomous Controls and Electrical subgroup in Wisconsin Autonomous, I have developed my leadership skills, along with becoming knowledgeable in the expanding field of autonomous navigation. Additionally, I am the Industry Chair for the Engineering EXPO, the largest student-led engineering showcase in the United States. As the Industry Chair, I am tasked with sponsor acquisition and relations. This past year, $15,000+ was raised through our corporate partnerships. In Insight Wisconsin, the premier interdisciplinary organization on campus, I was involved with three separate projects. Through these projects, I have had to work with strict deadlines and client requirements in an interpersonal setting.

During the 2019 summer, I developed valuable formal work experience as an undergraduate researcher for the Simulation-Based Engineering Lab (SBEL). This lab has provided me with essential experiences pivotal to my professional growth. Luckily enough, the work done in SBEL parallels the development for Wisconsin Racing, allowing me to further deepen my understanding of control stack implementations and autonomous vehicles.

In my free time, I enjoy being active, watching sports and traveling. A long term goal I hope to accomplish by September 2020 is completing my first IronMan. As a result, I workout and go for runs often. Recently I have developed an interest in cooking. In the future, I plan on living in an area where outdoor adventures are within reach; for instance, I can see myself living where there is an assortment of accessible hiking options.

Wisconsin Autonomous

Autonomous Controls and Electrical Lead

Wisconsin Autonomous is a student organization that develops a completely autonomus formula SAE vehicle for the Forumla Student Germany - Driverless competition in Germany. As Autonomous Controls and Electrical subgroup leader, I must allocate responsibilities to a group of 15 undergraduate and graduate students. Additionally, I must coordinate support with sponsors and oversee development of all controls elements, as well as, consult on vehicle progress. I emphasized my time on path planning algorithms and controls integration with the vehicle. As an undergraduate researcher in the Simulation-Based Engineering Lab, I developed an interface between our control stack and a physics simulation engine. This allowed us to test our algorithms completely independent of reality, using simulated sensory data. This was extremely pivotal in the success of our vehicle. We hope to compete in our first competition in August, 2020.

Simulation Based Engineering Lab

Undergraduate Researcher

The Simulation Based Engineering Lab (SBEL) at the University of Wisconsin - Madison is as forward thinking as it gets. As a major contributer to the open source physics engine named ProjectChrono, their research impacts a wide assortment of disciplines. As an undergraduate researcher, I was tasked to develop an API that interfaces the command line robotic control software known as Robotic Operating System (ROS), with ProjectChrono. Through my research, I learned valuable skills, such as C++ and ROS. Additionally, I worked collaboratively with other students and gained insightful knowledge about the professional workplace.

Insight Wisconsin

Member

As the premier interdisciplinary student organization on campus, Insight Wisconsin promoted innovative learning through collaborative team projects. As a member, I was participated in three separate groups: Timing Gate, Smart Showerhead and Plant Reader. In Timing Gate, I worked directly with UW Track and Field, developing a low cost and accurate timing system for their athletes. For Plant Reader, in collaboration with the UW Botany departement, I designed, proposed and implemented an electronic signal reader to measure the action potentials of plants such as the Mimosa Pudica. For all three projects, we utilized a variety of microcontrollers and designed housings using CAD software.

                          Path Planning

                          MatlabOptimization

                          This project was in conjunction with Wisconsin Autonomous. The task was to develop a path planning controller for our autonomous vehicle. The requirements were as follows: take an input of inner and outer bounds of a track and either develop a path that is subsequently followed or using a control loop, continuously optimize a an optimal path. The latter is the implementation chosen for the vehicle and what the above video demonstrates. This technique is known as Model Predictive Control (MPC) and utilizes optimization. Initalially written using Matlab, the final product will be C++.

                          ChROS

                          C++ROSOpenSourceProjectChrono

                          This project was in conjunction with the Simulation-Based Engineering Lab (SBEL). The primary research focus is in the OpenSource project called ProjectChrono. ProjectChrono is a hyper-realistic physics engine. It has an API for vehicle dynamics called Chrono::Vehicle. My task was to use this template and implement an interface that would allow for control of the vehicle through ROS. It could then be used for autonomous navigation, as sensor data could be created in the same form as it would in reality.
